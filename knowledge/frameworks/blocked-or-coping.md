# Blocked or Coping (PULL Framework)

Startup growth occurs when founders identify existing demand-side tension rather than creating demand via supply-side tactics. Success is finding customers who are already "sold" before encountering the product.

## Core Principle
- **Supply Side (Controlled):** Product, messaging, GTM tactics. Iterating here fails if demand-side PULL is absent
- **Demand Side (Uncontrolled):** Where PULL exists independently of the startup
- Goal: fit supply to existing demand, not change buyer priorities

## Prioritization States (The "Trello Board" Model)

Customers categorize goals into four states. PULL exists only in the middle two:

| State | Priority | PULL? |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Backlog** | Low priority | No |
| **Blocked** | Would prioritize now if viable options existed | **Yes** |
| **Coping** | Prioritizing now but struggling against current options | **Yes** |
| **Satisfied** | Prioritizing now with adequate options | No |

## Two Flavors of PULL

### Coping PULL

**Definition:**
- Buyer is actively working on a project but fighting against existing tools/methods
- They are struggling to minimize effort or friction

**Discovery Method: Observation**
- Watch buyers doing their jobs
- Look for visible friction, workarounds, manual processes, or complaints during execution

**Indicator:**
- Buyer perceives existing options as bad-fit but is forced to use them

### Blocked PULL

**Definition:**
- Buyer wants to prioritize a project but cannot act because existing options are unworkable or non-existent
- They are inactive regarding this goal

**Discovery Method: Inference & Questioning**
- Cannot be observed directly
- Infer from behavior (e.g., anxiety over a problem)
- Ask direct questions (e.g., "What would you pay me to solve that you currently cannot?")

**Indicator:**
- Hidden demand revealed only when a viable path is presented

## Validation Criteria

**Method:**
- Attempt to sell the solution. Observation alone is insufficient

**Success Signal:**
- "Dam bursting" energy
- Immediate attempt to purchase ("OMG how do I get started")

**Failure Signal:**
- Mild interest ("That could be helpful") or hesitation
- Indicates insufficient tension

## Key Risks

**Coping Risk: "Bitchin' ain't switchin'"**
- Users may complain about current tools but lack motivation to switch
- Validation requires observing purchase energy, not just frustration

**Blocked Risk: Wish-casting**
- Founders may assume a problem exists based on logic rather than actual buyer priority
- Validation requires pre-selling to confirm the hidden priority is real
