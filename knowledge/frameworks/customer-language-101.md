# Customer Language 101

## Core Problem
Customers speak "Customer Language" - a mix of demand, supply, and noise that mimics founder language but carries different meanings. Misinterpretation leads to building unwanted products.

## Demand vs. Supply Separation

### Demand Side (Trust These)
- Goals and outcomes customers are trying to achieve
- Even if vaguely stated, trust the underlying objective
- Represents what they're actually trying to accomplish

### Supply Side (Selective Trust)
- **Trust:** Complaints about current tools/solutions
- **Do NOT trust:** Stated solution preferences or feature requests
- Customers know their problem, not your solution

## Key Steps

1. **Isolate signals** - Separate demand (goals) from supply (tools) in customer monologues
2. **Identify underlying objective** - Look past stated solutions to the real problem
3. **Validate against demand** - Test solutions against the core demand, not stated preferences

## Critical Rules

- **Do not build based on stated supply requests** - Feature requests ≠ validated demand
- **Complaints ≠ Purchase Intent** - "Bitchin' ain't switchin'" - complaining doesn't guarantee they'll buy
- **Focus on the demand problem** - Solve the underlying goal, not the requested feature

## Application

Use during customer interviews to:
- Decode what customers actually need vs. what they say they want
- Prevent misaligned development from surface-level requests
- Validate that demand exists before building solutions
