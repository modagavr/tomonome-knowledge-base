# Customer Understanding Through Observation

## Core Principle: Forward Deploy Everyone
- Every team member (engineering, GTM) must work alongside customers
- Firsthand observation creates shared context across departments
- Passive interviews/recordings are insufficient - observe actual behavior

## The Customer Model

### The Calendar (Ground Truth)
- What customers actually do minute-by-minute
- Observable actions and behaviors

### The To-Do List (Intentions)
- What customers are trying to accomplish
- Product-agnostic goals (not "buy X")
- Their mental model of priorities

### The Conflict Zone (Opportunity)
Product opportunities exist where dissonance occurs:
- **Action Mismatch:** Customer doing something that doesn't fit their mental to-do list
- **Blocker:** Customer wants to prioritize an item but is blocked

## PULL Hypothesis Framework

Articulates who will aggressively adopt your product:

1. **Project:** Specific to-do list item they're trying to accomplish
2. **Situation:** Why this is urgent/unavoidable now (priority over backlog)
3. **Options:** Current alternatives considered (DIY, hire, competitors)
4. **Limitations:** Why existing options fail to fit the project

## Methods

### Shadowing
- Observe both buyers/champions (drive purchase/renewal) and users (drive usage/blockers)
- Ask to shadow customers directly (offer to observe work for research)
- Consider taking jobs/consulting roles with target customers for deep immersion

### Validation
- Test PULL hypotheses via sales sprint
- Use Jobs to Be Done (JTBD) for reverse-engineering purchase causality
- Use Spotlight Framework for analyzing unexpected customer feedback

## Avoid
- Vague heuristics like "pain points," "increase revenue," or "10x product" without behavioral evidence
