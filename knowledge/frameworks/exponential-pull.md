# Exponential Pull

Demand intensity ("PULL") is logarithmic, not linear. A small increase in intensity represents an exponential increase in demand power.

## Core Thesis
- Startup growth velocity is determined by who exerts the effort in the transaction
- **High-Intensity PULL:** Buyer exerts most of the energy to purchase
- **Low-Intensity PULL:** Seller must expend exponentially more energy to secure each customer
- It is easier to build a fast-growing startup than a slow-growing one because high-intensity PULL powers the engine via customer energy

## The PULL Scale

**No-Intensity:**
- Absence of priority/need
- Buyer exerts zero energy; seller effort is futile

**High-Intensity:**
- Buyer is "spring-loaded"
- They rip the product from the seller's hands
- They exert most pre- and post-sale effort

**Logarithmic Effort:**
- For each step down the intensity scale, the seller must perform ~10x more work to convert a customer
- Treating demand as linear is a critical error; only extreme intensity enables exponential growth

## Effort Distribution
- Total effort required for a purchase (pre- and post-sale) is split between buyer and seller
- High PULL shifts the burden to the buyer
- High-intensity PULL compensates for operational sins and attracts talent/capital
- Low-intensity PULL requires perfect execution just to achieve modest growth

## Diagnostic Method

**Analyze Sales Calls:**
- Measure buyer force in conversations
- Assess who is overcoming friction: the buyer (High PULL) or the seller (Low PULL)

**Iteration Process:**
1. **Identify Clues:** Look for anomalies indicating higher PULL (e.g., customer buying weirdly fast, specific moments of intense leaning-in during pitches)
2. **Form Hypothesis:** Create a new PULL hypothesis based on these clues
3. **Unfold Towards Intensity:** Adjust ICPs, messaging, or product to align with the high-intensity signal
4. **Repeat:** Continue iterating until buyer effort dominates the transaction

## Founder Reality
- Most founders start with low/no PULL
- The primary job is to navigate from moderate PULL to intense PULL using signals from early interactions
