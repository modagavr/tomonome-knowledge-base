# GTM Strategy by Deal Size

## ACV-Based GTM Motion

| ACV Range | Touch Level | Key Activities |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| < €10K | Low-Touch | Automation, high volume, broad awareness campaigns |
| €20K-€100K | Mid-Touch | Balanced automation + personalized outreach |
| > €250K | High-Touch | Highly personalized, consultative, executive engagement |

## GTM Activities by Touch Level

### Low-Touch (ACV < €10K)
- Awareness campaigns (social ads, SEM, content marketing)
- Lightweight content (blog posts, infographics, short videos)
- Community resources (forums, social media groups)
- Emphasize velocity and ease of deployment

### Mid-Touch (ACV €20K-€100K)
- Trigger-based outreach (website visits, content downloads)
- Educational resources (white papers, webinars, case studies)
- Structured follow-up process
- Spread through events and webinars

### High-Touch (ACV > €250K)
- Customized proposals and presentations
- Exclusive offerings (bespoke solutions, premium support)
- Executive engagement (C-level interactions)
- Brand-driven with expertise as key talking point
- Spread through networking and CxO referrals

## TAM Size Shapes Strategy

| TAM Size | Target Accounts | Strategy |
|----------|-----------------|----------|
| Small | 50-1,000 | Map entire market precisely, account-based programs, relationship depth |
| Medium | 2,000-10,000 | Balance targeting with scale, mix account-based and broader outreach |
| Large | 10,000+ | Efficient filtering, use intent data to prioritize, automate initial outreach |

## LTV:CAC Ratio

- Healthy ratio: At least 3:1 (earn $3 for every $1 spent on acquisition)
- Key driver of CAC is talent expense
- Monitor consistently to ensure profitable and sustainable growth

## Beachhead Strategy

- Don't try to conquer entire market at once
- Pick ideal customer segment, conquer and fortify it
- Use as launchpad for adjacent segments
- Example: Amazon mastered books first, then expanded category by category
