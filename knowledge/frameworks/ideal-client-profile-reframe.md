# Ideal Client Profile (ICP) Reframe

A reframe for identifying your true Ideal Client Profile based on **demand-side strategy** rather than supply-side assumptions.

## Core Problem

Standard ICP advice fails because it assumes **startups choose customers**, ignoring actual demand. Defining ICP based on who *could* benefit leads to:

- Sales friction ("pushing" vs. "pulling")
- Low conversion rates
- Random feature requests
- Slow decision cycles

These are **demand issues**, not product or sales skill issues.

## Reframed ICP Definition

**True ICP**: Customers who would be **weird not to buy** upon hearing about you.

The ICP is not who you *want* to sell to, but who is in a specific demand situation where your solution is the **obvious choice** — allowing the customer to choose the startup naturally without sales pressure.

## ICP Formula

```
ICP = A certain kind of person + A certain kind of situation
```

Where the situation includes:
1. **Specific project** on their to-do list (being prioritized now)
2. **Bad existing options** (current solutions are unworkable)

## Validation Metrics

True ICP identification is validated by:

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Conversion Rate | ~75%+ without persuasion |
| Post-Sale Satisfaction | ~95%+ success rate |
| Sales Cycle | Fast, customer-initiated momentum |
| Effort | Customer exerts effort to close |

## Push vs. Pull Sales

| Push (Wrong ICP) | Pull (Right ICP) |
|------------------|------------------|
| Convincing required | Customer self-qualifies |
| Low conversion | ~100% closed-won |
| Slow decisions | Fast decisions |
| Feature requests | Clear requirements |
| Sales friction | Natural momentum |

## Strategic Applications

### Market Definition
- PULL defines the "shape" of current market
- Identifies who will pull at t=now
- Focus on demand state, not demographics

### Sales Process
1. **Discovery**: Identify customers with specific project + urgency
2. **Qualification**: Verify existing options are unworkable
3. **Close**: Let customer initiate momentum (verify the pull)

### Product Design
- Pricing strategy aligned with urgency
- Onboarding experience for motivated buyers
- Success metrics that match customer goals

## Key Insights

**Inverse-Causality**
- Don't define ICP → find demand → build product
- Find demand → define ICP → align product

**"Weird Not to Buy" Criterion**
- The ultimate test of ICP fit
- Customer mindset: "This is obvious"
- No convincing needed

**Demand is Supply-Agnostic**
- Demand = what person is trying to achieve
- Not a desire for a specific product
- Focus on the job-to-be-done
