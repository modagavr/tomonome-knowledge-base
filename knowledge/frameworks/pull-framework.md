# PULL Framework

A framework for identifying customers in a state of **blocked demand** — those who would be "weird not to buy" your product.

## Framework Elements

**P — Project**
- Specific project on the customer's to-do list being prioritized now

**U — Urgent/Unavoidable**
- Reason the project is urgent vs. infinite backlog
- Customer's situation makes it "weird" not to prioritize

**L — List of Options**
- Options considered to accomplish the project
- Includes: direct competitors, alternative technologies, hiring, DIY

**L — Limitations**
- Severe limitations of considered options
- Prevents customer from making desired progress

## Core Concepts

**PULL Equation**
- (Project + Urgent) = Demand
- (List + Limitations) = Supply options blocking demand

**Definition**
- State where an unavoidable project meets unworkable options
- Customer mindset: would be "weird not to buy" your product

**Demand vs. Supply**
- Demand is supply-agnostic (what person is trying to achieve)
- Not a desire for a specific product

**Key Distinction**
- Different from generic "problems" or "pain points"
- Requires specific urgency + blocked progress
- Not abstract suffering

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Definition**: ICP = those with PULL

**Traits of PULL Customers**
- Buy without convincing
- ~100% closed-won rates
- ~100% post-sale success
- Fast sales cycles
- Customer exerts effort to close

**Opportunity Cost of Non-PULL Customers**
- Lower conversion rates
- Slower decision cycles
- Random feature requests

## Strategic Applications

**Market Definition**
- PULL defines the "shape" of current market
- Identifies who will pull at t=now

**Product Design**
- Dictates pricing strategy
- Shapes onboarding experience
- Defines success metrics
- Sets renewal requirements

**Sales Process**
1. **Discovery**: Fill out customer's PULL framework
2. **Offer**: Present supply that fits their PULL (nothing more, nothing less)
3. **Close**: Verify the pull (customer should initiate momentum)
