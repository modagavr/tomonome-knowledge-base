# Spotlight Framework for Customer Feedback

Companies often misinterpret customer feedback by focusing on the subject matter (the "what") rather than the structure of the inquiry (the "how"). The phrasing of customer questions reveals the underlying business issue.

## Core Principle
- Ignore the subject: Do not fixate on the specific variable (X, Y, Z) mentioned in the feedback—the subject is often a symptom, not the root cause
- Analyze the structure: The first half of the question determines the correct category
- Use categorization to direct time and energy to the correct business function rather than reacting superficially

## Three Categories of Feedback

### User Experience

**Indicators:**
- "How do I...?"
- "What happens when...?"
- "I tried to..."

**Core Issue:**
- Customers are struggling to use the product

**Focus Area:**
- Product usability and interface

### Product Marketing

**Indicators:**
- "Can you/I...?"
- "How do you compare to...?"
- "How are you different than...?"
- "Why should I use you for/to...?"

**Core Issue:**
- Customers do not understand the product's capabilities or value proposition

**Focus Area:**
- Communication of features and benefits

### Positioning

**Indicators:**
- "I'm probably not your target customer, but..."
- "I'm sure I'm wrong, but I thought..."

**Core Issue:**
- Customers do not believe the product is intended for them

**Focus Area:**
- Market segmentation and brand positioning

## Application

**Objective:** Transform vague feedback into actionable strategy by identifying whether the barrier is:
- **Usage** (UX) → Improve product interface
- **Understanding** (Marketing) → Improve communication
- **Relevance** (Positioning) → Improve market segmentation

**Benefit:** Prevents misallocation of resources (e.g., building features when the issue is actually marketing)
