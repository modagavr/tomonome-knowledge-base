# How to Describe Your Product Quickly

Effective product description must begin with the customer's immediate context and limitation (PULL) before introducing the solution (Supply).

## Core Principle
- Starting with technology, features, or broad visions confuses buyers
- Goal: fit the buyer's current situation with the fewest words possible so they can immediately understand the value and say "yes"
- You do not get points for extra words or fancy technologies—only for fitting PULL

## The Correct Sequence (Infomercial Structure)

### 1. Articulate PULL (Context & Limitation)
Define the user's attempted action and the specific blocker preventing success.

**Format:** "Whenever you are trying to [ACTION]... your existing supply [LIMITATION] prevents you from [ACTION]."

**Purpose:** Sets context so the brain is prepared to understand the solution

### 2. Introduce Supply That Fits PULL
State Product Name + Category + How it removes the specific limitation.

**Format:** "[Product Name], the [Category] that [enables action without limitation]."

**Purpose:** Maps the solution directly to the previously stated context

### 3. Demonstrate How It Fits PULL
Show the process of using the product to accomplish the action without the limitation.

**Focus:** Answer "How does this enable me to accomplish X without Y?" (Not technical mechanics)

### 4. Unique Supply-Side Mechanism (Optional)
Explain proprietary technology only in terms of what it enables relative to the PULL.

**Constraint:** Do not explain technical mechanics unless they clarify the benefit to the PULL

## Key Definitions

**PULL:**
- Not a static "pain point" (e.g., "heavy")
- A dynamic attempt to accomplish something blocked by a specific limitation (e.g., "Trying to fill gas, but the can spills")

**"How It Works":**
- In this framework, this means "How does this fit my situation?"
- Not "What are the components?" or "How does the technology function?"

## The Startup Anti-Pattern

**Incorrect Order:**
Unique Tech → Technical Mechanics → Supply Features → Customer Context

**Result:**
- Pitches are massive, broad, future-oriented, and technical
- Confuses buyers who are trying to accomplish something *now*

## Success Metric
Fast-growing companies say very few words and demo only things that fit the buyer's PULL
