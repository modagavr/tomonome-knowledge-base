# Buyer-Pull vs Seller-Push Sales Methodology

The primary force closing a deal must be the buyer's need to accomplish a specific item on their to-do list (BUYER-PULL), not the seller's effort to convince (SELLER-PUSH).

## Core Principle
- PUSH creates friction; PULL aligns with existing demand
- Any moment requiring "push" indicates misalignment in product, messaging, or targeting
- PUSH efforts compound friction; PULL efforts compound ease

## Comparison Across Sales Process

### Pipeline & Targeting
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Target anyone who could derive value or has pain points | Target prospects for whom it would be **weird not to buy** given their current priorities |

### Messaging
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Attempt to convince prospects they have demand/problems (low response rates) | Acknowledge existing demand; offer conversation valuable to their current goals |

### Call 1: Demand (Discovery)
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Interrogate to surface pain points; build logical case to buy | Identify specific projects on their to-do list. Ask why this project now vs. others. Confirm: "You are trying to accomplish X because Y, and current options fail because Z" |

### Call 1: Supply (Positioning)
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Describe product broadly/ambitiously (VC-style); explain history and full capabilities | Describe product narrowly as the specific solution to their identified PULL. Fit supply to demand |

### Call 1: Demo
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Walk through features/workflows to prove power (10-20+ minutes) | Show only what fits their PULL (30-60 seconds). Demonstrate outcomes/outputs, not features |

### Call 2 to Close
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Standard sales steps (deep dives, business cases) to convince stakeholders | Treat as **project management**. Remove work from champion's plate. Design process based on minimum steps *they* need to see/do to buy |

### Onboarding & Customer Success
| PUSH | PULL |
|------|------|
| Drive general product adoption/usage; success defined by vendor metrics | Drive completion of their specific project. Success is when canceling feels irrational because their goal was achieved |

## Key Insights

**Product-Market Fit Indicator:**
- PMF is achieved when customers consistently pull through the entire process without resistance (repeating one case study)

**Diagnostic Signal:**
- Any moment requiring "push" indicates misalignment

**Scope:**
- Optimized for B2B SaaS ($10k-$100k ACV) but adaptable for enterprise, self-service, or non-software products
