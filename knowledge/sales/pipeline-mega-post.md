# Pipeline Mega Post

Pipeline shortages are frequently misdiagnosed as Product-Market Fit crises. The root cause is insufficient customer contact.

## Core Principle
- Schedule 1-2 meetings per day (5-10 per week)
- Dedicate first 2-3 hours of workday exclusively to scheduling
- Selling is identifying those who already have demand, not convincing people to buy

## Phase 1: Unknown Pull (Pre-PMF) → Founder Magic

**Goal:** Secure conversations without a proven value prop

**Tactics:**
- Pitch founder uniqueness/weirdness instead of product ROI
- Make declining the meeting seem irrational
- Adjust messaging/profiles every 1-2 weeks until meeting targets are met
- "Goblin Mode": Employ extreme, non-scalable tactics (physical visits, gifts) if standard outreach fails

**Avoid:**
- Traditional sales emails (ROI-focused) fail pre-PMF — they exist in uncanny valley between personal outreach and sales spam

## Phase 2: Known Pull (Post-PMF) → Toll Booth

**Prerequisite:** Defined "Pull Equation" (identified ICP who would be "weird not to buy") after 5-10+ customers

**Goal:** Scalable growth (50+ meetings/week, $10M+ ARR)

**Tactics:**
- Identify and dominate the choke point where demand inevitably exists
- Angle 1: Own the situation where the project is unavoidable
- Angle 2: Own the moment prospects realize alternatives are unworkable

## Valid vs Invalid Hacks

**Valid:**
- Paid advisor intros (equity for qualified introductions) can source significant early pipeline

**Invalid:**
- Revenue swapping with other startups creates false metrics

## Transition Point
Switch from Founder Magic to Toll Booth only after demonstrating repeatable pull with 5-10+ customers
