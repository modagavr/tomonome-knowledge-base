# Sales Sprint for 0-1 Sales

0-1 sales success depends on identifying customers who are "weird not to buy" through rapid, low-fidelity experimentation.

## Core Principle
- Find repeatability by validating demand before building product
- Do not use traditional enterprise sales playbooks, pitch competitions, or product-led growth hopes
- Goal: identify customers who need no convincing and rip the product out of your hands

## The Sales Sprint Method

### Step 1: Craft Hypothesis
- Define who would be "weird not to buy" using the PULL Framework
- This forms your initial Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Step 2: Schedule 5 Conversations
- Secure meetings with people fitting the hypothesis
- Outreach should sell the conversation, not the product
- Be interesting/weird; do whatever it takes

### Step 3: Execute Conversations
- Test the PULL hypothesis
- Do not pitch, persuade, or demo heavily
- Collaborate to verify if they fit the hypothesis and have demand

### Step 4: Analyze & Iterate
- Compare outcomes across calls
- Determine if demand existed and if supply fit
- Adjust the hypothesis or target segment and run another sprint

## Key Metrics

**Sample Size:**
- 5 conversations is optimal for proof without waste (acceptable range: 3-8)

**Velocity:**
- Complete a sprint in 2 days to 2 weeks

**Call Objective:**
- Verify if the prospect fits the PULL hypothesis, not to close a deal immediately

## Success & Failure Signals

**Success Signal:**
- Customers who need no convincing
- They rip the product out of your hands
- They find success without hand-holding

**Failure Signal:**
- Cannot fill out the PULL framework based on a real person
- Solution: go find a real person in-person rather than using interviews or AI research
