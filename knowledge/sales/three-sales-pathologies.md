# Three Sales Pathologies (Helpful, Noble, Smart)

Sales calls fail when founders optimize for self-validation (appearing helpful, smart, or noble) rather than the customer's progress. Potential customers are solely focused on accomplishing items on their to-do lists.

## Core Principle
- A successful sales call must ignore the seller's ego/mission and focus exclusively on the progress the customer is trying to make
- These counterproductive behaviors cause ghosting and low close rates

## Correct Sales Call Structure

1. **Intro:** Determine why they took the call (identify the specific progress they are trying to make)
2. **Demand Discovery:** Understand their PULL (literally fill out the PULL framework with them)
3. **Supply:** Fit their PULL (offer a solution that fits better than their existing alternatives)
4. **Next Steps:** Define immediate actions

## The Three Pathologies

### The "Helpful" Pathology

**Behavior:**
- Problem-solving live during the call to prove value or partnership

**Failure Mode:**
- Solving vectors irrelevant to the customer's actual to-do list
- Providing answers to questions they did not ask

### The "Smart" Pathology

**Behavior:**
- Setting agendas without asking
- Inferring problems instead of asking
- Giving educational presentations
- Answering all questions immediately
- Demoing everything to avoid looking stupid

**Failure Mode:**
- Making logical leaps instead of understanding where the customer is
- Missing the customer's actual PULL by pitching assumed needs

### The "Noble" Pathology

**Behavior:**
- Focusing the pitch on the company's mission, "why," or global impact

**Failure Mode:**
- Making the call about the seller rather than the customer
- Customers may feel inspired but will not buy because the mission does not address their immediate to-do list
