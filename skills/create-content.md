# Skill: Create Content and Social Media Posts from Knowledge Base

## Objective
Transform knowledge base content into engaging social media posts and content optimized for specific platforms and audiences.

---

## Input
- Knowledge base file(s) or topic
- Target platform(s): LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, etc.
- Content type: post, thread, carousel, article
- Audience: founders, sales reps, marketers, etc.

---

## Content Strategy Foundation

### Content Mission Statement
Before creating content, define its strategic purpose:

```
Our content exists to [TRANSFORM] our [ICP] from [CURRENT STATE] to [DESIRED STATE] by [MECHANISM], which drives [BUSINESS OUTCOME].
```

**Example:**
> Our content exists to transform mid-market RevOps leaders from reactive firefighters to strategic architects by providing implementation frameworks and data-backed decision models, which drives qualified demo requests and product-led signups.

### Content Differentiation Thesis
Pick 1-2 differentiation vectors:
- **Depth**: 10x deeper than competitors (comprehensive guides vs. surface-level)
- **Data**: Original research, proprietary benchmarks, first-party insights
- **Format innovation**: Visual, interactive, or structural approaches others don't use
- **Perspective**: Contrarian, insider, or underserved audience viewpoint
- **Velocity**: Cover breaking topics/trends faster than anyone
- **Practitioner credibility**: Content from people doing the work, not marketers observing
- **Community integration**: User-generated, crowdsourced, or co-created content

### Content Philosophy: Educational vs. Promotional
Position your content on the spectrum:

```
Pure Education ←—————[Your position]—————→ Pure Promotion
(Never mention product)              (Every piece sells)
```

**Guidelines by business model:**
- **Long sales cycle/High consideration**: 90% education, 10% promotion
- **Product-led growth**: 70% education, 30% product demonstration
- **Transactional/Impulse**: 50% education, 50% promotion

### Content-to-Revenue Mapping
Map content types to funnel stages:

| Funnel Stage | Content Type | Business Metric | Target Conversion |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Awareness** | Thought leadership, trend analysis, industry research | Net new visitors, social reach, backlinks | Visitor → Subscriber |
| **Interest** | Educational guides, comparison content, use cases | Time on site, pages per session, return visitors | Subscriber → MQL |
| **Consideration** | Product education, ROI calculators, customer stories, demos | Trial signups, demo requests, pricing page visits | MQL → SQL |
| **Decision** | Case studies, comparison pages, security/compliance docs | Contract proposals, pricing negotiations | SQL → Customer |
| **Retention & Expansion** | Onboarding guides, feature tutorials, best practices, community | Product adoption, NPS, renewal rate, expansion ARR | Customer → Advocate |

**Resource allocation example (Early stage):**
- 50% Awareness, 30% Interest, 15% Consideration, 5% Decision

## Strategic Marketing Architecture for Content Planning

Before executing content creation, understand the broader go-to-market context that shapes content strategy.

### Context Initialization

**Startup Profile:**

- Company: [Name and 1-sentence value proposition]
- Stage: [Pre-product/MVP/Early revenue/Growth stage]
- Industry/Category: [Specific vertical + whether you're creating new category or competing in existing one]
- Product/Service: [What you're selling and the core problem it solves]
- Current state: [Team size, runway, monthly burn, current revenue if any, user/customer count]

**Target Market Fundamentals:**

- Primary customer segment: [Be specific - not "small businesses" but "5-50 person B2B SaaS companies in fintech with $2M-$10M ARR"]
- Customer's current alternative: [What are they doing today instead of using your product?]
- Economic buyer vs end user: [Who signs the check vs who uses the product daily?]
- Deal size & sales cycle: [Average contract value and typical time from first touch to closed deal]

### Strategic Foundation Development

#### Phase 1: Market Position & Narrative Architecture

Develop the strategic positioning that will guide all content decisions:

1. **Category Strategy Decision**
    - Are you competing in an existing category or creating a new one?
    - If existing: What's your differentiated position? (Don't say "better features" - what's the philosophical difference?)
    - If new category: What's the incumbent behavior you're replacing? What's your "X for Y" bridge concept?

2. **Value Proposition Hierarchy**
    Articulate at three levels:
    - **Functional value**: The literal capability/outcome (quantified if possible)
    - **Economic value**: The ROI/cost savings/revenue impact (with realistic calculation methodology)
    - **Emotional/strategic value**: The status, risk mitigation, or strategic advantage (what gets the champion promoted?)

3. **Competitive Moat Identification**
    What prevents copycats in months 6-18? Options:
    - Network effects (requires specific mechanics)
    - Proprietary data/IP (must be actually defensible)
    - Brand/community (takes years to build)
    - Operational complexity (sustainable if you have unfair advantage)
    - Regulatory barriers (double-edged sword)
    
    **Critical constraint**: Your moat must be realistic for your stage. Pre-revenue startups don't have moats yet - identify your *path* to a moat.

4. **Messaging Framework**
    Construct the narrative stack:
    - **Big shift**: What macro trend makes this the right time? (AI adoption, remote work, regulatory change, etc.)
    - **Problem crystallization**: Make the pain vivid and urgent - why does status quo fail *now*?
    - **Your unique insight**: What do you understand about the market/customer that incumbents miss?
    - **Solution introduction**: Present your product as inevitable consequence of your insight
    - **Proof points**: What evidence builds credibility at your stage? (Design partners, early metrics, team credentials, existing traction)

#### Phase 2: Channel Strategy Selection

Analyze each channel through startup-appropriate lens:

**For B2B/SaaS:**

1. **Founder-led Sales** (Essential for first 10-50 customers)
    - Ideal customer profile: [Specific firmographics + psychographics]
    - Outbound methodology: [Personalized email/LinkedIn/warm intros - pick one to master first]
    - Conversion milestones: [Reply rate → meeting rate → demo-to-trial → trial-to-paid]
    - What founder learns: [What objections reveal about product-market fit]

2. **Content/SEO** (3-12 month payoff)
    - Search intent mapping: What do customers Google when experiencing the problem?
    - Content formats: [Prioritize bottom-funnel "how to [solve problem]" over thought leadership initially]
    - Distribution strategy: [Where does your audience actually consume content?]
    - Realistic timeline: [Month-by-month traffic projections based on domain authority + competitive analysis]

3. **Product-Led Growth** (If applicable)
    - Self-serve feasibility: [Can users activate without sales involvement?]
    - Viral/network mechanics: [Does product get better with more users? Is there native sharing?]
    - Monetization moment: [At what point of value delivery do you ask for money?]
    - PLG math: [Conversion rates at each funnel stage + CAC payback period]

4. **Partnerships/Integrations**
    - Strategic rationale: [Does partner have your customers and distribution?]
    - Value exchange: [What do you give them beyond revenue share?]
    - Integration depth: [Listed in marketplace vs deep native integration]
    - Reality check: [Enterprise partnerships take 6-18 months - do you have runway?]

5. **Paid Acquisition**
    - Channel selection: [Google/LinkedIn/Facebook - pick based on where buying decisions happen]
    - Unit economics requirement: [LTV must be 3x+ CAC for venture-scale, 5x+ for bootstrapped]
    - Testing budget: [$5-10K minimum to get signal in B2B, $2-5K for B2C]
    - Scaling threshold: [At what CAC/conversion rate does channel become primary?]

**Channel Prioritization Framework:**

Rank each channel by:

- **Time to first customer**: [Days/weeks/months]
- **Scalability ceiling**: [Can this get you to $1M ARR? $10M?]
- **Capital efficiency**: [Cost per qualified lead/customer]
- **Learnings value**: [Does this channel teach you about product-market fit?]
- **Founder skill fit**: [Do you have unfair advantage in this channel?]

**Strategic recommendation**: Pick ONE primary channel and ONE secondary. Startups die from channel diffusion.

#### Phase 3: 90-Day Campaign Architecture

Design your content campaigns with built-in learning loops:

**Sprint 1 (Days 1-30): Signal Generation**

- **Goal**: Validate that target customer segment recognizes problem as urgent
- **Tactics**:
    - [Primary channel]: [Specific campaign with measurable KPI]
    - [Secondary channel]: [Specific campaign with measurable KPI]
- **Success metrics**: [Response rate, meeting booking rate, quality of conversations]
- **Kill criteria**: [At what point do you pivot segment/message?]

**Sprint 2 (Days 31-60): Conversion Optimization**

- **Goal**: Move interested prospects through to product trial/demo
- **Tactics**:
    - Refine messaging based on Sprint 1 objections
    - Develop demo/trial enablement: [Sales deck, demo environment, onboarding sequence]
    - Create proof points: [Case studies, ROI calculator, comparison pages]
- **Success metrics**: [Demo-to-trial rate, trial activation rate, time-to-value]
- **Optimization focus**: [Where is friction highest in funnel?]

**Sprint 3 (Days 61-90): Early Scaling**

- **Goal**: Achieve predictable customer acquisition motion
- **Tactics**:
    - Double down on highest-converting channel
    - Systematize outreach/follow-up
    - Begin content/SEO foundation for longer-term plays
- **Success metrics**: [MQL→SQL→Customer conversion rates, CAC, sales cycle length]
- **Scale decision**: [At what point do you hire first marketing/sales person?]

#### Phase 4: Metrics & Financial Framework

**Marketing Dashboard Requirements**

Track metrics at three levels:

**1. Vanity Metrics** (directionally interesting, not actionable)

- Website traffic, social followers, content views
- **Use case**: Board updates and morale, not decision-making

**2. Health Metrics** (indicate channel/message effectiveness)

- Email open/reply rates, ad CTR, landing page conversion rate
- Content engagement time, demo request rate, trial activation rate
- **Use case**: Tactical optimization of campaigns

**3. Business Metrics** (actual startup survival indicators)

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by channel
- Payback period (months to recover CAC)
- Magic Number (net new ARR / sales & marketing spend)
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) with realistic churn assumptions
- **Use case**: Strategic resource allocation and fundraising narrative

**Financial Discipline Protocol:**

For each marketing investment, calculate:

```
Expected CAC = (Channel spend + allocated time cost) / Expected customers
LTV/CAC ratio = (Average contract value × expected lifetime) / CAC
Payback period = CAC / (Monthly recurring revenue per customer)
```

**Decision rule**:

- Venture-backed: LTV/CAC > 3x, payback < 12 months
- Bootstrapped: LTV/CAC > 5x, payback < 6 months

#### Phase 5: Risk Mitigation & Constraint Acknowledgment

**Common Startup Marketing Failure Modes**

Address these explicitly:

1. **Premature scaling**: Spending on acquisition before product-market fit is proven
    - **Prevention**: Set strict "PMF gates" (retention rate, NPS, organic referrals)
    - **Rule**: No paid acquisition scale until 3+ months of cohort retention data

2. **Diffusion of effort**: Trying every channel simultaneously
    - **Prevention**: Written channel prioritization with kill criteria
    - **Rule**: Maximum 2 active channels until one generates 50%+ of pipeline

3. **Vanity metric optimization**: Optimizing for traffic/impressions over revenue
    - **Prevention**: Compensation and OKRs tied to revenue, not awareness
    - **Rule**: Every campaign must have projected ROI calculation upfront

4. **Founder bottleneck**: All marketing depends on founder's personal network
    - **Prevention**: Document and systematize what founder does that works
    - **Rule**: Build repeatable process by customer 20, not customer 100

5. **Message-market misalignment**: Saying what you want to say vs what customer needs to hear
    - **Prevention**: Record every sales call, analyze objections weekly
    - **Rule**: Message testing with 20+ target customers before campaign launch

### Content Strategy Output Requirements

Generate a comprehensive content strategy document including:

**1. Executive Summary** (1 page)

- Market opportunity and positioning
- Primary customer segment and their urgent problem
- Go-to-market motion (channel + campaign approach)
- 90-day targets and resource requirements
- Key risks and mitigation strategies

**2. Strategic Foundation** (2-3 pages)

- Complete messaging framework with proof points
- Competitive positioning and moat strategy
- Channel selection rationale with prioritization
- Unit economics model and assumptions

**3. 90-Day Execution Plan** (2-3 pages)

- Week-by-week campaign calendar
- Specific tactics with owners and budgets
- Success metrics and kill criteria for each initiative
- Resource requirements (people, tools, budget)

**4. Measurement Framework** (1 page)

- Marketing dashboard with realistic targets
- CAC/LTV model by channel
- Weekly review cadence and decision triggers

**5. Constraint & Risk Register** (1 page)

- Top 5 risks to plan execution
- Mitigation strategies and contingency plans
- Realistic assessment of what you don't know yet

**Critical Success Factors:**

This strategy should:

- ✓ Be implementable with your actual team and budget
- ✓ Generate learnings even if campaigns "fail"
- ✓ Have clear go/no-go decision points based on data
- ✓ Acknowledge uncertainty while maintaining direction
- ✓ Focus on revenue impact, not marketing theater

**The ultimate test**: Could you show this to a seasoned CMO or venture investor and have them say "this person understands startup marketing dynamics"?

### Idea Scoring Rubric
Score each content idea 1-100 before production:

**BUSINESS IMPACT (40% weight)**
- Funnel alignment: Does this map to priority funnel stage?
- ICP relevance: How central is this to ICP's needs?
- Conversion potential: Likelihood to drive MQLs/trials?
- Sales enablement: Will sales team use this?

**TRAFFIC POTENTIAL (30% weight)**
- Search volume: Monthly searches for target keyword
- Keyword difficulty: Ranking feasibility
- Ranking opportunity: Can we realistically rank page 1?

**EFFICIENCY (20% weight)**
- Resource requirement: Time/effort to produce
- SME availability: Access to internal experts
- Leverage potential: Can this be repurposed?

**STRATEGIC VALUE (10% weight)**
- Differentiation: How unique vs competition?
- Authority building: Does this establish expertise?

**Priority thresholds:**
- **80-100**: Produce this quarter
- **60-79**: Backlog for next quarter
- **40-59**: Revisit if strategic priorities change
- **<40**: Reject/archive

- Deadline: 24-48 hours
- Focus: Technical accuracy, missing nuances, insider perspective
- SME is NOT rewriting, just validating expertise

**Stage 3: Editorial Review (Editor/Content lead)**
- Deadline: 24 hours after SME feedback
- Focus: Structure, clarity, flow, brand voice, SEO optimization
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Headline optimized (keyword + compelling)
  - [ ] Introduction hooks within 2 sentences
  - [ ] Subheadings are descriptive (H2/H3 hierarchy correct)
  - [ ] Paragraphs are scannable (3-5 sentences max)
  - [ ] Examples/data support claims
  - [ ] CTAs are clear and contextual
  - [ ] Internal links added (3-5 relevant pieces)
  - [ ] Keyword naturally integrated

**Stage 4: Design/Visual Production**
- Deadline: 24-48 hours (can overlap with editorial)
- Deliverables: Hero image, supporting graphics, social media assets

**Stage 5: Final Review & SEO Check**
- Deadline: 24 hours before publication
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Image alt text added
  - [ ] URL slug optimized (short, keyword-rich)
  - [ ] Meta description written (150-160 characters)
  - [ ] Mobile preview checked
  - [ ] All links working

## Content Performance & Optimization

### CTA Strategy by Funnel Stage

| Funnel Stage | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA | Product Mention |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Awareness** | Newsletter signup, social follow | Related content ("Read next") | No (too aggressive) |
| **Interest** | Gated asset (template, checklist) | Newsletter signup | Subtle ("Our customers use...") |
| **Consideration** | Free trial, demo, consultation | Product-focused content | Central to content |
| **Decision** | Talk to sales, start trial | Multiple strong CTAs | Entire piece is product-centric |

### Conversion Optimization Checklist

**For every published piece:**
- [ ] CTA is appropriate for funnel stage
- [ ] CTA copy is specific, not generic ("Download guide" not "Click here")
- [ ] CTA stands out visually (button, color contrast, whitespace)
- [ ] CTA appears multiple times if content is long (>1,500 words)
- [ ] Exit-intent popup configured (for high-value content)
- [ ] Internal links to 3-5 related pieces (keep readers on site)
- [ ] Lead magnet relevant to content topic (if gated)
- [ ] Thank you page includes next step (not dead end)
- [ ] Email follow-up sequence configured (if form submission)

### Performance Metrics Dashboard

**Track at three levels:**

#### Individual Content Piece Metrics (Monitor weekly):

**Traffic Metrics:**
- Total sessions (organic + referral + social + direct)
- Organic traffic specifically
- Entrances (how often it's landing page)
- Traffic trend (week-over-week %)

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Average time on page
- Scroll depth (% reaching bottom)
- Bounce rate
- Pages per session (from this piece)
- Return visitor rate

**Conversion Metrics:**
- CTA clicks
- Form submissions
- Trial/demo requests
- Email signups

**SEO Metrics:**
- Keyword rankings (primary + secondary keywords)
- Position changes week-over-week
- Backlinks acquired
- Referring domains

#### Performance Tiers & Actions:

| Tier | Criteria | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Star Performer** (Top 10%) | >30% of total organic traffic OR >25% of content-attributed conversions OR >50% of backlinks | Invest in refresh, create cluster content, repurpose heavily |
| **Solid Performer** (Next 30%) | Consistent traffic and engagement, acceptable conversion rates | Maintain, update annually, continue promotion |
| **Underperformer** (Bottom 50%) | Low traffic despite time, high bounce rate, no ranking movement | Diagnose issue, refresh if strategic value, otherwise archive |

### Optimization Protocols

**Weekly optimization (every Monday, 2-3 hours):**
- Review previous week's publications: traffic patterns, early ranking movements
- Identify quick wins: improve meta descriptions, add internal links, fix technical issues

**Monthly deep dive (first week of month, 4-6 hours):**
- Full performance review of all active content
- Identify refresh candidates (performance decline)
- Analyze keyword ranking opportunities (page 2+ ranking where page 1 is achievable)
- Competitive content gap analysis

**Quarterly strategic review (end of Q, 1 full day):**
- Aggregate content performance vs business goals
- ROI analysis: content investment vs pipeline/revenue influenced
- Strategy refinement: Double down on what works, cut what doesn't
- Annual editorial calendar planning for next quarter

### Content Refresh Protocol

**Refresh triggers:**

| Priority | Triggers | Timeline |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Factual error, broken links, product/pricing changes, legal/compliance issue | Within 1 week |
| **Priority** | Traffic decline >50% MoM, ranking drop from page 1 to 2+, competitor publishes superior content | Within 1 month |
| **Standard** | Top 20 traffic-driving pieces, pieces ranking positions 4-10, pillar content | Quarterly review |

**Archive criteria:**
- <100 organic visits in last 12 months
- No backlinks
- Not ranking for any keywords
- Information permanently obsolete

**Refresh process:**
1. **Audit** current content (read through, check facts, test links)
2. **Update** content (refresh stats, add new sections, remove outdated info)
3. **Optimize** for performance (expand thin sections, improve title/meta, strengthen CTAs)
4. **Republish & Re-promote** (update date, share on social, email to subscribers)
5. **Measure** impact (track ranking changes, monitor traffic recovery)

## Content Backlog Management

### Backlog Structure (90-day rolling pipeline)

| Time Horizon | Status | Characteristics |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Committed** (Next 30 days) | Fully briefed, assigned, in production | Publication dates set, no changes unless critical |
| **Planned** (Days 31-60) | Topics decided, priority scored | Briefs in development, flexible but directionally committed |
| **Pipeline** (Days 61-90) | Ideas captured, scored | Subject to reprioritization based on business needs |

### Backlog Management Tool Requirements:
- Kanban view (Backlog → Briefing → Writing → Review → Scheduled → Published)
- Priority scoring and sorting
- Deadline tracking
- Assignee management
- Progress visibility

**Tool options:** Notion, Airtable, Trello, Asana, Monday.com, Spreadsheet (early-stage)

### Backlog Hygiene:
- **Weekly:** Add 5-10 new ideas from customer conversations, keyword research, trends
- **Monthly:** Rescore pipeline based on changing priorities, new data
- **Quarterly:** Major backlog refresh, archive stale ideas, re-prioritize based on performance

### Capacity Planning

**Calculate realistic content output:**

```
Available content hours per week = 
  (FTE writers × 40 hours × % time on content creation) +
  (Contractor hours) +
  (SME contributor hours)

Realistically (accounting for meetings, distribution, optimization):
  = ~80% of calculated capacity
```

**Example:**
- 1 FTE content writer (80% time on creation) = 32 hours/week
- 1 contractor = 20 hours/week
- SME time (founder + CS lead) = 5 hours/week
- **Total: 57 hours/week → ~45 usable hours/week**
- **Production capacity: 3 standard articles/week or 12-15/month**

### Publication Frequency Recommendations:

| Stage | Team Size | Cadence | Focus |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Early stage** | 0-2 people on content | 2-4 posts/month (1/week) | Quality over quantity, build authority |
| **Growth stage** | Dedicated content person/team | 8-16 posts/month (2-4/week) | SEO momentum, consistent cadence |
| **Scale stage** | Content team + freelancers | 20+ posts/month (1+/day) | Topic domination, channel diversification |

### Calendar Management Practices:
- **Finalize 2 weeks ahead:** No last-minute scrambles
- **Build 4-week buffer:** Always have 1 month of content ready to publish if production stalls
- **Theme weeks/months:** Cluster related content for topic depth + internal linking
- **Leave 20% flexible:** Room for timely/reactive content without disrupting schedule
- **Review weekly:** Every Friday, review next week's calendar and adjust if needed
